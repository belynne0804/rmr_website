Rehabilitation Services

Vocational Assessment and Testing

Assessment is an important part of the rehabilitation process. The purpose is to accurately understand the strengths and limitations to a person’s employment. From a person’s transition into the workforce, to career changes, to exiting the workforce, each person’s situations is unique and requires an accurate understanding of both individual factors as well as labor market barriers to employment. Rocky Mountain Rehab specializes in assessment of employability, earning capacity, and labor market availability.

Program Administration

Rocky Mountain Rehab currently supports the North Dakota Division of Vocational Rehabilitation extended employment program by providing case management tools and supervision. For individual’s with significant disabilities, the supported employment program provides ongoing intervention both on and off the job for those in need. We have also managed the Wounded Warrior program for the state of Montana.

Medical and Vocational Case Management

Case management is the process of providing ongoing support to 1.) establish an effective medical and vocational rehabilitation plan and 2.) using resources wisely and effectively to achieve their individual goals. Counseling and case management often are combined to support individuals through the complicated and often overwhelming process of finding and maintaining employment
